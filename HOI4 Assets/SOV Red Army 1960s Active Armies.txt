On 20 May 1957 the 5th Guards Tank Army was reformed from the 5th Guards Mechanized Army. It was stationed in the Belarussian Military District until 1992.[5] Throughout the postwar period it had an almost constant composition of three tank divisions â€“ the 8th Guards (Osipovichi) and 29th Tank Division (former tank corps) (Slutsk, and the 193rd Tank Division (Bobruisk). The headquarters was located in Bobruisk. On 21 February 1974, the army was awarded the Order of the Red Banner . In August 1979, the 84th Motor Rifle Division (a mobilization unit) was attached to the army at Marina Gorka. It was disbanded in 1987.[5]
